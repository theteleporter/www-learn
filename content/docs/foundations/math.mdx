## Why Math Matters

Math is the quiet power behind every algorithm, simulation, and circuit.  
You don’t need to be a mathematician — just fluent enough to make sense of the world you’re building.  
This is about *thinking precisely* and *modeling reality*.

<div className="fd-steps [&_h3]:fd-step">

### Step 1 – Algebra & Equations
Learn how to express relationships between things — variables, constraints, and unknowns.  
This is how you model systems and predict outcomes.  
**Focus on:** linear equations, functions, inequalities.  

**Explore more:**  
- [Khan Academy – Algebra 1](https://www.khanacademy.org/math/algebra)  
- [Paul’s Online Math Notes](https://tutorial.math.lamar.edu/)  

---

### Step 2 – Geometry & Trig
The world has shape — learn how to describe it.  
From angles in robotics to vector math in graphics, geometry builds your spatial intuition.  
**Focus on:** triangles, vectors, coordinate systems, transformations.  

**Explore more:**  
- [Brilliant – Geometry Fundamentals](https://brilliant.org/courses/geometry-fundamentals/)  
- [3Blue1Brown – Essence of Geometry (YouTube)](https://www.youtube.com/@3blue1brown)

---

### Step 3 – Calculus (Without Crying)
Change is constant — calculus helps you model it.  
Understand how things move, grow, and react in systems.  
**Focus on:** limits, derivatives, integrals, and differential equations (conceptually first).  

**Explore more:**  
- [MIT OCW – Calculus 1](https://ocw.mit.edu/courses/mathematics/18-01sc-single-variable-calculus-fall-2010/)  
- [3Blue1Brown – Essence of Calculus (YouTube)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WUvTyaaNkzM)

---

### Step 4 – Probability & Statistics
Engineering isn’t all certainty — sometimes you just play the odds.  
Probability helps you reason about randomness, while stats help you make sense of data.  
**Focus on:** distributions, expected value, correlation, and regression.  

**Explore more:**  
- [Khan Academy – Statistics & Probability](https://www.khanacademy.org/math/statistics-probability)  
- [StatQuest – YouTube Channel](https://www.youtube.com/@statquest)

---

### Step 5 – Discrete Math
This is the math computers *actually* care about.  
Learn about sets, graphs, combinatorics, and logic structures — the backbone of computer science.  

**Explore more:**  
- [Discrete Math by Shai Simonson (YouTube)](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLUl4u3cNGP61MdtwGTqZA0MreSaDybji8)  
- [Brilliant – Discrete Mathematics](https://brilliant.org/courses/discrete-mathematics/)

## Wrap Up

Math isn’t a subject — it’s a toolkit.  
Every concept you just saw ties into something real you’ll build later: circuits, simulations, AI models, even UI animations.  
Don’t rush it. Learn to *see* with math, not just calculate.
